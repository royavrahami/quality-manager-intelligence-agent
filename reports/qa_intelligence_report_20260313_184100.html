Influencing LLM Multi-Agent Dialogue via Policy-Parameterized Prompts
🕵️ AI Agents
The paper explores the use of policy-parameterized prompts in Large Language Model (LLM) based multi-agent systems to influence dialogue without the need for training. It proposes a framework where prompts are treated as actions executed by LLMs, constructed dynamically based on the agent's current state. The effectiveness of this approach is evaluated using five indicators: responsiveness, rebuttal, evidence usage, non-repetition, and stance shift. The study demonstrates that parameterized prompts can effectively alter dialogue dynamics, providing a new direction for social simulation research in multi-agent systems.
